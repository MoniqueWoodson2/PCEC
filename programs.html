Phillips Brotherhood Community Outreach (P.B.C.O.)

Phillips Brotherhood Community Outreach

P.B.C.O. mobilizes men and boys to serve as role models and servant-leaders, building character, life skills, and stronger neighborhoods.

PBCO Activities

  • Neighborhood clean-ups and food distribution
  • Life skills and financial literacy workshops
  • Mentor-mentee fellowship events
  • Home repair support for seniors

Leadership & Service

Structured mentoring and service projects help young men discover purpose, resilience, and integrity at school, work, and home.

PBCO Activities

  • Career panels with local professionals
  • Reading buddies and tutoring
  • Sportsmanship and teamwork clinics
  • Quarterly service days

Women Made Whole (W.M.W.)

Women Made Whole

W.M.W. offers community, resources, and skills training so women can heal, grow, and lead—uplifting families and communities.

WMW Activities

  • Support groups and wellness circles
  • Career coaching and entrepreneurship labs
  • Parenting, budgeting, and home management workshops
  • Community care projects for mothers and caregivers

Empowerment & Wellness

From practical workshops to restorative events, W.M.W. equips women to navigate life transitions with confidence and connection.

WMW Activities

  • Résumé clinics and mock interviews
  • Health and wellness seminars
  • Resource navigation: housing, childcare, education
  • Volunteer service with local partners

Young At Heart (Y.A.H.)

Young At Heart

Y.A.H. supports seniors through fellowship, wellness, and learning—spaces where older adults are honored, engaged, and connected.

YAH Activities

  • Monthly luncheons and birthday celebrations
  • Technology help sessions
  • Blood pressure checks and wellness education
  • Outings to museums, gardens, and concerts

Connection & Care

With volunteers by their side, seniors explore new interests, stay active, and receive practical support with dignity and joy.

YAH Activities

  • Friendly phone calls and check-ins
  • Transportation assistance for appointments
  • Reading circles and crafts
  • Community service projects

Youth Mentoring

Purpose, Belonging, Growth

Students connect with caring adults for academic support, life planning, leadership experiences, and positive peer communities.

Sample Activities

  • Homework help and academic coaching
  • STEM clubs and career exploration
  • Leadership retreats and college visits
  • Service learning and community showcases

Mentor Engagement

Mentors receive practical training and ongoing support to help youth set goals, build skills, and navigate challenges.
